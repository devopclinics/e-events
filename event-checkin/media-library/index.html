What the customer actually sees

Channel and rate are internal-only. The customer only ever sees plain "credits" and dollars.

SurfaceCustomer sees
Public pricing pageOne line under the Event Pass grid: "Messaging (SMS, WhatsApp, and more) is pay-as-you-go — top up inside your event dashboard." No numbers, no channels.
Billing panelA credit balance ("175 credits remaining") and "Buy credits" buttons ("100 credits — $50", "500 credits — $250", "2,000 credits — $1,000") — no channel name anywhere.
Before sending a broadcastA single estimated cost in dollars plus recipient count ("$150 for 300 recipients"), no per-channel breakdown.

Internally the system can still use one fungible "credit" as its unit of account (an implementation detail, see §8) — the customer never sees per-channel math.

Message bundles

Customer-facing label is just "buy credits" — no channel name, no per-message rate shown. Internally each price point still maps to a real per-channel rate (§2), it's just never surfaced. Flat per-unit price at every size — no volume discount below the $0.50-equivalent floor.

Customer seesPriceInternal mapping (not shown)

A credit still spends at a worse "exchange rate" on pricier channels (WhatsApp/MMS/marketing) under the hood — the customer just sees their balance go down faster on those sends, never why.

Open decisions

Resolved items are marked ✓. Everything else carries a recommendation.

  1. ✓ Resolved — Refund policy: no refunds, ever. Once a send is attempted, the credit is spent — regardless of provider outcome, immediate rejection, or later delivery failure.
  2. Guest-capacity add-on blocks — which tiers can buy them? Recommend: Scale-only. Otherwise a cheap tier plus guest blocks can undercut Scale's price for a similar guest count.
  3. Advanced Access & Zone Control wording. Needs the sentence written: "included at Standard+; this add-on is the deeper Venue Access Intelligence layer."
  4. Email — truly unlimited, or a soft cap? Recommend: unlimited in customer-facing language, invisible anti-abuse rate limit underneath.
  5. Existing customers / already-granted credits. Recommend: grandfather in place — no retroactive clawback or conversion.
  6. Bundle/credit expiry. Recommend: keep the 30-days-post-event expiry.
  7. ✓ Resolved — NGN pricing is a straight FX conversion of the finalized USD figures, decided once USD is locked in.
  8. Custom domain annual renewal. Recommend: $49/year.
  9. "From $149" items base scope. Recommend: define a concrete floor for each — e.g. sender setup covers registration + 3 templates, $25/template after; import covers one clean file up to 2,000 records.
  10. Top-up amounts. Recommend: keep $50 / $250 / $1,000 as-is.
  11. Pre-send cost estimate. Recommend: show both dollar amount and recipient count.
  12. ✓ Resolved — Deliveries/Gift/FestioMe/Guest Communication are now flat per-event service charges ($50/$50/$50/$10 — see §4), no longer included free at Starter+.
